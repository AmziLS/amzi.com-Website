TIBCO and Amzi!:
Implementing Efficient Business Rules Applications
with Prolog Scripting

TIBCO Software is rapidly expanding to become the leader in middleware applications. It was named one of "The 12 Most Influential Companies in IT" by CMP net's Intelligent Enterprise feature story, "The Dozen 2001,” and is poised to become a leader in Enterprise Application Integration.

TIBCO’s premier application for the management of workflow using intelligent business rules is InConcert, a member of the ActiveEnterprise application suite. InConcert coordinates both workflow, and human and electronic resources, through a flexible and scalable graphical interface (see figure). InConcert thus allows managers and team members to see and manage complex workflow patterns, thus increasing efficiency and customer satisfaction, and improving team moral.

But unlike traditional project management tools, InConcert is rules-based. This moves workflow processing from a simple dependency check-list into a robust environment that can handle the simultaneous and interleaved activities found in real-world workplaces. Rules give InConcert the ability to adjust the workflow on-the-fly as new problems or situations are encountered.

A simple example using InConcert is scheduling the installation of new phone service. If a telephone junction box has a maximum of five lines, and a sixth line is ordered, then a new junction box must be installed before the new phone service can be started. A single instance of this kind of rule-based decision process may appear trivial. But, phone installations have hundreds of such rules that represent a myriad of personnel and equipment constraints. InConcert can reason over all those constraints for each individual customer and identify which ones are applicable to that customer's situation. Then, InConcert can reason over the resources available to solve the problems, and plan and manage the optimum workflow to get the job done. InConcert’s ability to organize workflow scheduling can improve both operational efficiency and customer satisfaction.

These workflow issues occur in any large business, whether the tasks being ordered and managed are phone installations, stocktrades, or any other industry specific process. InConcert is designed to have the flexibility and expandability to fit into any business setting, and to work efficiently for any large organization, by building and customizing applications to meet the customer needs within the structure and resources of the organization.

Prolog as the Scripting Language

TIBCO’s choice of Amzi! Prolog as a rules scripting language provides much of InConcert’s power, flexibility, and ease of use. Choosing Prolog has also raised some eyebrows in an industry where the obvious scripting choice might have seemed to be Perl or Javascript.  The three basic misconceptions about the use of Prolog for business rules applications were:

  1. That the amount of shelf space given Prolog in the average developer’s library was extremely small, compared to Javascript or Perl.
  2. There is a smaller number of experienced Prolog scripters (again, relative to Perl or Javascript).
  3. Some lingering misunderstanding of the effectiveness of Prolog for business applications, given the long association of Prolog with AI.

The first two of these reservations were answered by simply introducing the developers to Prolog. Since Amzi! Prolog source code can be run directly, there is no compilation phase required (although it is available). And Prolog is compact, easy to read with straightforward syntax. Once programmers overcame the initial hurdle of Prolog’s apparently unusual structure, they are often amazed at how much they can accomplish without extensive training or practice. New users at InConcert's training classes are typing in usable business rules before the lunch break

The third concern, that “Prolog is an AI language” was answered because the capabilities of Prolog were an obvious match with InConcert's product requirements. Prolog is a language for flexible and powerful searching and pattern matching. This has led to Prolog’s success in a variety of rule-based business applications, which was a significant factor in TIBCO’s choice of Prolog for InConcert's process engine.

Other options were initially considered, but all were found to be “brittle”, in the words of the project’s chief developer, Ed Black, “When you reach the edge, you just fall off.” Neither other scripting languages, nor a spreadsheet approach, had the necessary power or flexibility. And using Prolog as a scripting language led to significantly shorter implementation time for InConcert than would have been possible using a more conventional scripting choice. Even a Javascript expert, on examining the problem statements for InConcert concluded that “…Prolog was a much better fit” for the application.

Choosing Amzi! Prolog

Of the various implementations of Prolog available, TIBCO chose Amzi! Prolog for several reasons. Amzi! has been in business for over a decade, and has an industry wide reputation for close and extremely productive working relationships with clients. TIBCO’s developers speak enthusiastically about Amzi!’s “almost instantaneous support”.

On the technical side, Amzi! Prolog proved the best fit, both because of its flexibility and its ability to be extended with functions in other programming languages via Logic Server Extensions (LSX’s). The Logic Server Extensions allow Amzi! Prolog to be the heart of InConcert's process engine. The process engine takes embedability to a new level by having the LSX's (written in C++) call back to Amzi! Prolog. (So you have C++ calling Prolog which in turn calls C++ which calls Prolog!)

In addition, InConcert uses the full power of the Amzi! Logic Server by having multiple servers (running under multiple threads) in its process engine, each providing a different set of specialized workflow services. Some of these services include enacting tasks and listening for and acting on messages. The servers communicate with each other using an internal messaging system.

Another reason TIBCO selected Amzi! is its Logic Server which allows Prolog modules to be embedded in many languages, for InConcert this is Java and C++. Amzi! also can run on almost any platform. This gives InConcert the ability to ship its product on HP/UX, Solaris, and Windows.

For developers, the Amzi! Integrated Development Environment is, in the words Ed Black, “fast and flexible”, allowing for “very rapid applications development.” And Amzi! Prolog is reliable. It is described by the TIBCO team as “the most robust part of the application".

Prolog for Business Rules Scripting

TIBCO’s choice of Prolog was, as described above, a leap in thinking about Enterprise Application Integration. Prolog was not the usual choice, or the obvious one. But InConcert has proved the usefulness, value and speed of Prolog in the development of business rules applications.

This is good news for TIBCO’s clients, who are able to purchase fast, flexible, and robust software, which can be adapted to a wide range of industries and companies.

And this is good news for the Prolog community, offering more evidence of the flexibility and power of Prolog, the logical and quickly learned scripting language for business.
