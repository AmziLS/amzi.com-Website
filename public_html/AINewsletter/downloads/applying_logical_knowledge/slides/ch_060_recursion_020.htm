  Path

Recursion, in any language is specified in two steps:

  • The boundary condition, or simple degenerate case
  • The recursive condition.

In Prolog these often become two rules. Ex, finding the various connections from an East coast city.

  • Boundary - there is a flight from here to there.
  • Recursive - there is a flight from here to somewhere in between, and it connects to there
connect(FROM, TO) :-
   flight_ew(FROM, TO).
connect(FROM, TO) :-
   flight_ew(FROM, X),
   connect(X, TO).
