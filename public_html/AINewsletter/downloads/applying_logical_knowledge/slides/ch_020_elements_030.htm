Applying Logical Knowledge

  Clauses

Clauses are logic program building blocks. They can be facts or rules.

Fact

Syntactically - A structure or atom, followed by a period (.)
Semantically - a fact is always true. 

flight('Delta 432', 'Asheville', 'Miami').

customer( 27, name('Smith', 'Fred'), vehicle(432) ).

vehicle( 432, [make = 'Suburu', model = 'Forester', color = blue] ).

something_always_true.

Rule

Syntactically - A fact, called the head, followed by :-, called the neck, followed by comma separated conditions, called the body, followed by a period (.)
Semantically - The head of a rule is true only if the conditions of the body are true.

customer(X, good) :- account_balance(X, paid).

bird(hawk) :- has(talons), flies(fast), vision(excellent).

car_color(CustID, Color) :-
  customer(CustID, _, vehicle(VehicleID)),
  vehicle(VehicleID, PropertyList),
  member(color = Color, PropertyList).

A fact is really a rule with no body; or more precisely a body which is always true.
