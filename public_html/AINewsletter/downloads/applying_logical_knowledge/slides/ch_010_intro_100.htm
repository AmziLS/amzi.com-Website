  Performance

In the 1980s Prolog had a reputation as being slow.

It was, relatively speaking.

Today, Prolog is blindingly fast, relatively speaking.

Prolog hasn't changed much, but machines are much much faster, operating environments are much more cumbersome, and networking is still often the bottle neck.

Example: Rubik's cube demo - 4-5 minutes in mid 1980s, about 20 cubes/second on this machine today.
